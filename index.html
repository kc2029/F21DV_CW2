Percentage of UK population that play video games

Gaming has become a highly prevalent leisure activity in the UK, especially among young adults aged between 16-24 years. Current data reveals that around 70% of individuals in this age group engage in video games as a form of entertainment. The Covid-19 pandemic and the ensuing lockdown measures have caused a remarkable surge in the number of individuals who play video games, peaking at over 90%. Even after the pandemic, the percentage of people who continue to play video games remains significantly higher compared to pre-pandemic levels, signifying a maintained interest in this popular pastime.

Overview of UK gaming industry

The UK gaming industry is a global leader, ranking as the 6th largest in the world and the largest in Europe. In 2022, it was estimated to be worth $7.55 billion, employing over 50,000 people in the UK and contributing £2.9 billion to the UK economy annually. Some of the world's most renowned video game companies, including Rockstar Games with Grand Theft Auto and Red dead Redemption, and Rare studio, known for Donkey Kong Country, Banjo-Kazooie, and GoldenEye 007, are based in the UK.

The UK gaming industry is predicted to continue its growth trajectory in the next five years, reaching over $11 billion by 2027.

Revenue split of the UK gaming Industry

The UK gaming industry has seen a steady increase in revenue over the past few years. A significant portion of this revenue can be attributed to the rising popularity of loot boxes and microtransactions in games. Loot boxes is a virtual lottery with a chance to get a random in-game items (often cosmetics) of various rarity. It has become a controversial topic in the gaming community due to their exploitative nature especially toward childing and young adult. Despite this, the revenue generated by these features continues to grow, with some estimates suggesting that loot boxes alone generated over £700 million in the UK on 2019.

From the graph, it is evident that the usage of loot boxes in games has significantly increased over the past 8 years. Initially, loot boxes were present in less than 10% of games, but it has risen to 70% in just 8 years as game developers realising the lucrative potential of exploiting gamers' gambling tendencies.

However, this trend has also raised concerns about the potential harm caused to individuals with gambling disorders, particularly since loot boxes have been compared to virtual gambling. And there is growing calls for the UK govement to place ban on Loot box.
